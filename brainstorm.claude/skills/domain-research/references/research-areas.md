# Research Areas Taxonomy

This document defines the 7 research dimensions used in domain research analysis.

## 1. Market Landscape Analysis

Understanding the overall market structure, maturity, trends, and key players.

**What to Look For**:

- Industry maturity level (emerging, growing, mature, declining)
- Market size and growth trajectory
- Key market players and their market share
- Market trends and emerging opportunities
- Barriers to entry and competitive dynamics

**Questions to Answer**:

- What is the current state of the market?
- Who are the major players?
- What trends are shaping the market?
- What opportunities exist for new entrants?

**Sources to Consult**:

- Industry reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
- Market research databases
- Trade publications
- Social listening and trend analysis platforms

## 2. Competitive Analysis

Evaluating direct and adjacent competitors to understand positioning and differentiation.

**What to Look For**:

- Direct competitors (same problem, same market)
- Adjacent solutions (related problems or markets)
- Pricing models and business strategies
- Market positioning and messaging
- Feature comparison and gaps

**Questions to Answer**:

- Who are the direct competitors?
- What differentiates each competitor?
- How should we position relative to competitors?
- What pricing models work in this space?
- Are there open source or free alternatives?

**Sources to Consult**:

- Competitor websites and documentation
- Product review sites (G2, Capterra)
- Job postings (insight into company strategy)
- Press releases and news articles

## 3. Best Practices

Industry standards, proven patterns, common approaches, and anti-patterns to avoid.

**What to Look For**:

- Industry-standard approaches and methodologies
- Best-in-class implementations
- Proven patterns and frameworks
- Known anti-patterns and pitfalls
- Emerging or evolving best practices

**Questions to Answer**:

- What approaches have proven most effective?
- What pitfalls should we avoid?
- Are there emerging or evolving best practices?
- What standards and frameworks apply?

**Sources to Consult**:

- Technical documentation and guides
- Open source implementations
- Conference talks and presentations
- Expert blogs and articles
- Technical books on the domain

## 4. User Expectations

What users expect from solutions in this domain based on existing experiences.

**What to Look For**:

- Expected features and capabilities
- Common workflows and use cases
- Pain points and frustrations with current solutions
- Delight opportunities (unexpected value)
- Expected levels of support and service

**Questions to Answer**:

- What features do users expect as baseline?
- How do users expect to accomplish key tasks?
- What are the common complaints about existing solutions?
- What would delight users beyond expectations?

**Sources to Consult**:

- User reviews and feedback
- Social media discussions
- Support forums and Q&A sites
- User testing and observational research

## 5. Technical Patterns

Common architectures, data models, and integration approaches in the domain.

**What to Look For**:

- Common system architectures and designs
- Standard data models and structures
- Integration patterns and APIs
- Technology choices and frameworks
- Performance characteristics and requirements

**Questions to Answer**:

- What architectures are commonly used?
- How do systems typically integrate?
- What API standards are followed?
- What technologies are most commonly used?
- What performance characteristics are expected?

**Sources to Consult**:

- Technical documentation and APIs
- Open source projects and their source code
- Architecture blogs and case studies
- Developer community discussions

## 6. Regulatory and Compliance

Legal requirements, privacy obligations, and compliance standards.

**What to Look For**:

- Applicable regulations and compliance frameworks
- Privacy and data protection requirements
- Industry-specific compliance standards
- Audit and reporting requirements
- Geographic variations in requirements

**Questions to Answer**:

- What regulations apply to this domain?
- What are the data privacy requirements?
- How do requirements vary by geography?
- What are the penalties for non-compliance?

**Sources to Consult**:

- Regulatory agency websites and guidance
- Compliance frameworks (GDPR, HIPAA, SOC 2, etc.)
- Legal and compliance blogs
- Industry associations

## 7. Domain Terminology and Conventions

Industry vocabulary, concepts, and standard terminology.

**What to Look For**:

- Standard terminology and definitions
- Common terminology variations and synonyms
- Concepts and frameworks unique to the domain
- Acronyms and abbreviations
- Regional or cultural variations in terminology

**Questions to Answer**:

- What is the precise terminology for key concepts?
- How do terms vary across regions or contexts?
- What mental models do users have?
- How should concepts be named and organized?

**Sources to Consult**:

- Glossaries and terminology guides
- Industry standards documentation
- Domain experts and practitioners
- Academic literature

## Integration Summary

These seven areas work together to provide comprehensive domain understanding:

1. **Market Landscape** sets the context for the domain
2. **Competitive Analysis** shows what's already being done
3. **Best Practices** illuminate the proven approaches
4. **User Expectations** define the bar for success
5. **Technical Patterns** guide implementation decisions
6. **Regulatory/Compliance** set hard constraints
7. **Domain Terminology** ensures clear communication

Use all seven areas in concert to develop a complete understanding of the domain
before making design decisions.
