# Research Areas Taxonomy

This document defines the 7 research dimensions used in domain research analysis.

## 1. Market Landscape Analysis

### Definition

### What to Look For

- Industry maturity level (emerging, growing, mature, declining)
- Market size and growth rates
- Major trends and shifts
- Key players and market leaders
- Market segments and opportunities
- Geographic variations

### Questions to Answer

- How large is the market and how fast is it growing?
- What are the dominant trends shaping the industry?
- Who are the market leaders and what positions do they hold?
- Are there emerging niches or sub-segments?

- What barriers exist to entry?

### Sources to Consult

- Industry analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
- Market research firms
- Industry publications and news
- Trade associations and standards bodies
- Academic research and white papers
- Social listening and trend analysis platforms

---

## 2. Competitive Analysis

### Definition

Detailed examination of direct competitors, adjacent solutions, and industry leaders to identify
positioning opportunities and learn from their approaches.

- Direct competitors (solving same problem, same market)
- Adjacent solutions (related problems or markets)
- Open source alternatives and free solutions
- Industry leaders and best-in-class examples

- Feature comparison across solutions
- Pricing models and business strategies
- Market positioning and messaging
- Strengths and weaknesses of each competitor

### Questions to Answer

- Who are the direct competitors?
- What differentiates each competitor?
- Which features do all competitors have (table stakes)?

- Where do competitors fall short?
- What can we learn from each approach?
- How should we position relative to competitors?
- What pricing models work in this space?
- Are there open source or free alternatives?

### Sources to Consult

- Competitor websites and product documentation
- Customer reviews and ratings (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot)
- Product demos and trial experiences
- Press releases and announcements
- Job postings (insight into company strategy)
- SEC filings and financial reports (for public companies)

- Sales conversations and customer interviews
- GitHub and open source repositories

---

## 3. Best Practices and Patterns

### Definition

Industry standards, proven patterns, common approaches, and anti-patterns to avoid when implementing solutions.

### What to Look For

- Industry standards and specifications
- Established design patterns
- Common architecture approaches
- Best-in-class implementations
- Known anti-patterns and pitfalls
- Common workflow conventions
- Emerging best practices

### Questions to Answer

- What are the established standards in this domain?
- What design patterns are commonly used?
- What approaches have proven most effective?
- What pitfalls should we avoid?
- What do industry leaders do differently?
- Are there emerging or evolving best practices?
- What conventions do users expect?

### Sources to Consult

- Industry standards documents (ISO, NIST, etc.)
- Academic journals and research papers

- Technical documentation and guides
- Open source implementations
- Conference talks and presentation slides

- Expert blogs and articles
- Technical books on the domain
- Professional communities and forums

---

## 4. User Expectations and Behavior

### Definition

Understanding what users expect from solutions in this domain, including expected features, common pain points, and unmet needs.

### What to Look For

- Expected features (what users assume will be included)
- Common workflows and use cases
- User pain points and frustrations
- Unmet needs and opportunity gaps
- Expected levels of support and service

- Integration expectations
- User education and onboarding requirements

### Questions to Answer

- What features do users expect as baseline (table stakes)?
- What are the most common use cases?
- What are the biggest pain points users face?
- What do users wish existed?
- How do users expect to accomplish key tasks?
- What causes user frustration or churn?
- What level of support do users expect?
- How much customization do users need?

### Sources to Consult

- Customer interviews and user research

- Support tickets and customer feedback
- Online communities and forums (Reddit, Stack Overflow)
- User reviews and ratings (G2, Capterra)
- User surveys and questionnaires
- Job forums and professional networks
- User testing and observational research

---

## 5. Technical Insights and Patterns

### Definition

Understanding common technical architectures, data structures, integration patterns, and API conventions used in the domain.

### What to Look For

- Common system architectures and designs
- Standard data models and structures
- Typical integration patterns and protocols
- API conventions and standards
- Technology stack choices
- Performance and scalability approaches
- Security patterns and considerations
- Data format standards

### Questions to Answer

- What are the common architecture patterns?
- What data structures are standard?
- How do systems typically integrate?
- What API standards are followed?
- What technologies are most commonly used?

- What performance characteristics are expected?
- What security measures are critical?
- What data formats are standard?

### Sources to Consult

- Technical documentation and APIs
- Open source projects and their source code
- Architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Technical blog posts and articles
- GitHub repositories and code analysis
- Technical forums and Stack Overflow
- Conference talks and technical presentations

- Industry technical specifications
---

## 6. Regulatory and Compliance Requirements

### Definition

Understanding legal requirements, regulatory frameworks, privacy obligations, accessibility standards,
and security requirements that apply to solutions in this domain.

### What to Look For

- Applicable regulations and compliance frameworks
- Privacy and data protection requirements
- Accessibility mandates and standards
- Security certifications and requirements
- Industry-specific compliance standards
- Audit and reporting requirements
- Liability and insurance considerations
- Geographic variations in requirements

### Questions to Answer

- What regulations apply to this domain?

- What privacy standards must be met?
- What accessibility standards apply?
- What security certifications are needed?

- What audit and reporting is required?
- How do requirements vary by geography?
- What are the penalties for non-compliance?
- What do leading solutions do to ensure compliance?

### Sources to Consult

- Regulatory agency websites and guidance
- Compliance frameworks (GDPR, HIPAA, SOC 2, etc.)
- Legal counsel and compliance experts

- Industry association guidelines
- Competitor privacy policies and security pages
- Standards bodies (WCAG, NIST, etc.)
- Compliance tool documentation
- Legal and compliance blogs

---

## 7. Domain Terminology and Conventions

### Definition

Industry-specific vocabulary, terminology conventions, and expected concepts that users in the domain understand and expect.

### What to Look For

- Industry-specific terminology and jargon
- Term definitions and precise meanings
- Common terminology variations and synonyms
- Concepts and frameworks unique to the domain
- Naming conventions and standards
- Taxonomies and categorization schemes
- Acronyms and abbreviations

### Questions to Answer

- What is the precise terminology for key concepts?
- How do different sources use the same terms differently?
- What jargon is expected knowledge?
- Are there competing frameworks or models?
- What acronyms and abbreviations are standard?
- How do terms vary across regions or contexts?
- What mental models do users have?
- How should concepts be named and organized?

### Sources to Consult

- Industry glossaries and dictionaries
- Academic and research publications
- Industry standards documents
- Professional association resources
- Expert interviews
- Existing competitor documentation
- Domain-specific forums and communities
- Books and comprehensive guides on the domain

---

## Research Integration

These seven areas work together to provide comprehensive domain understanding:

1. **Market Landscape** provides the context and opportunity
2. **Competitive Analysis** shows what's already being done
3. **Best Practices** illuminate the proven approaches
4. **User Expectations** reveal what customers value
5. **Technical Insights** guide architectural decisions
6. **Compliance Requirements** ensure legal and regulatory safety
7. **Domain Terminology** ensures clear communication with users

Use all seven areas in concert to develop a complete understanding of the domain before making design decisions.
