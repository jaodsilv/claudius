# Domain Research

Explores industry practices, competitors, and domain knowledge to inform solution design with market context.

## When to Use

- Before solution design to understand market landscape
- When analyzing competitive landscape and market positioning
- To identify industry best practices, patterns, and standards
- During requirements gathering to validate market expectations
- To understand regulatory and compliance requirements

## Research Areas

The domain research skill covers 7 key research dimensions:

1. **Market Landscape** - Industry maturity, market size, trends, and key players
2. **Competitive Analysis** - Competitor features, positioning, strengths, and weaknesses
3. **Best Practices** - Industry standards, proven patterns, and anti-patterns
4. **User Expectations** - Expected features, pain points, and delight opportunities
5. **Technical Insights** - Common architectures, data models, and integrations
6. **Regulatory/Compliance** - Legal requirements, privacy, accessibility, and security
7. **Domain Terminology** - Industry vocabulary, conventions, and standard concepts

See `references/research-areas.md` for detailed definitions and research questions for each area.

## Output Format

Provide a structured analysis:

```
# Domain Research Report

## Executive Summary
- Domain: [Name]
- Key Findings: [2-3 main insights]
- Market Maturity: [Emerging/Growing/Mature/Declining]

## 1. Market Landscape
- Key players and market size
- Market trends and opportunities
- Identified gaps

## 2. Competitive Analysis
- Direct and adjacent competitors
- Feature comparison matrix
- Positioning recommendations

## 3. Best Practices
- Industry standards and patterns
- Anti-patterns to avoid
- Implementation considerations

## 4. User Expectations
- Expected features
- Common pain points
- Delight opportunities

## 5. Technical Patterns
- Common architectures
- Standard data models
- Integration expectations

## 6. Compliance Considerations
- Regulatory requirements
- Privacy and security standards
- Accessibility mandates

## 7. Sources & Gaps
- References and citations
- Areas needing further research
```

## Best Practices

- Cite sources when possible
- Distinguish facts from opinions and emerging trends
- Note when information may be dated
- Focus on actionable insights
- Compare approaches across multiple sources
- Connect research to specific requirements and recommendations
